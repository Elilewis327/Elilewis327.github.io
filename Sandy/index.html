General info
Hurricane Sandy, sometimes known as superstorm Sandy, was a massive weather event in october of 2012. The effects of the event include 233 casualties and around $70 billion in economic damages, Making it one of the top 10 most economically damaging storms since 1900 in the US. It is estimated to have damaged 650,000 structures. The event was well documented from its origin, and most of the entirety of the eastern US seaboard was locked down with shelters and support in place ahead of landfall of the event. The event caused storm surges and other flooding, as well as other extreme weather events. While it had downgraded into a 70 kt tropical cyclone by the time it hit the US coast, the sheer size of the storm caused it to have a massive impact. See the size, damages, and more below.

Conclusion
In conclusion, Hurricane Sandy was a large, deadly, and impactful storm. However, if not for the forward thinking and quick action of the Air Force and National Weather Service, the storm could have been much worse.
